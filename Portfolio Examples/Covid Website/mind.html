Tips for a Healthy Mental State

Here are a few things that you can do to keep a positive outlook during self isolation.

Nature.com gives 7 things to keep your mental health in a good state from Clinical Psycologist Desiree Dickerson.

  1. Manage your expectations
  2. Proactively manage your stress threshold
  3. Know your Red Flags
  4. Routine is your friend
  5. Be compassionate with yourself and with others
  6. Maintain connections
  7. Manage uncertainty by staying in the present

So with 7 things to look into I would like to focus on 3 main items that I find that help me in these times. Having a Routine, Be Compassionate with yourself and others and Managing my Expectations.

When creating a routine I try to give myself a few tasks each particular time of the day. I divided my time up to Early Morning, Morning, Afternoon, Early Evening and Evening. I schedule about 3-4 items that I try to complete or do to make sure I am being productive but also giving myself some needed away time, since I am working from home.

Being Compassionate to yourself and others is a great way to feel better about the situation you are in. In the news, social media and everyday life we are all dealing with the same issues and stress. How we take that stress and turn it into positive energy is up to each of us to figure it out. What does help is dealing with those around us with a lot of compassion. Knowing that everyone is going through the same thing should help us all realize that we are in this together. Being helpful, charitable and understanding with yourself is about giving yourself time to meditate - I highly recommend the App Headspace - and removing the stress from your mind will help give you some peace and help you fall asleep easier.

I find the best thing that you can do manage your expectations is by creating a mantra. I will link an article from Oprah.com. This has a recipe to create your own mantra. By creating your own mantra with the items that will help keep you in a positive frame of mind and focused on managing your life while stuck in your home.
